# Pug Grooming Guide: How to Clean Wrinkles, Manage Shedding, and Keep Your Pug Fresh

Pugs have a short coat. Easy grooming, right?

Not quite. Pug grooming is more involved than their low-maintenance appearance suggests — and the most important part has nothing to do with their coat. It's the wrinkles.

This guide covers the complete pug grooming routine: what needs doing daily, weekly, and monthly, plus how to do each task correctly.

---

## The Grooming Schedule at a Glance

| Task | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Wrinkle cleaning | Daily |
| Eye wiping | Daily |
| Teeth brushing | Daily |
| Coat brushing | Weekly (daily during shedding season) |
| Ear cleaning | Every 1–2 weeks |
| Bathing | Every 3–4 weeks |
| Nail trimming | Every 2–3 weeks |
| Professional grooming | Every 6–8 weeks (optional) |

## Wrinkle Cleaning: The Most Important Task

This is where pug grooming earns its reputation. Those adorable folds accumulate food, moisture, and debris — creating a warm, dark environment where bacteria and yeast thrive. Left uncleaned, the result is painful skin fold dermatitis (pyoderma).

### What You'll Need
- Dog-safe grooming wipes **or** cotton pads + warm water
- A dry cloth or cotton pad for drying
- Vet-prescribed antifungal/antibacterial wipe (for pugs prone to infection)

### Step-by-Step
1. Gently fold back each wrinkle one at a time
2. Wipe inside with a damp cloth or dog-safe wipe
3. Pay extra attention to the nose roll (the large fold above the nose) and the folds under the eyes
4. **Dry each fold thoroughly** — this step is just as important as cleaning. Residual moisture causes infection.
5. If you notice redness, odor, or discharge, consult your vet

**For pugs prone to recurring infections:** Ask your vet about prescription wipes containing chlorhexidine or ketoconazole. Daily use of these prevents flare-ups.

**The nose rope/roll:** The large fold directly above the nose is the most commonly neglected and the most prone to problems. Never skip it.

## Eye Care

Pugs' protruding eyes are prone to dryness, discharge, and injury. Daily eye care prevents problems from developing.

### Daily Eye Wipe
1. Dampen a soft cloth or cotton pad with warm water
2. Gently wipe from the inner corner of the eye outward
3. Use a fresh cotton pad for each eye (cross-contamination prevention)
4. Look for: redness, cloudiness, excessive discharge, squinting

**When to call the vet:**
- Green or yellow discharge (signs of infection)
- One eye significantly more closed than the other
- Visible cloudiness on the eyeball
- Any sign of eye trauma

**Lubricating eye drops:** Many pugs benefit from artificial tear drops, especially in dry climates or after being near fans/AC vents. Ask your vet for a recommendation.

## Teeth Brushing

Dental disease is one of the most common health problems in pugs. Daily brushing is the single most effective prevention.

### What You'll Need
- Dog-safe toothpaste (chicken, peanut butter, or vanilla flavors are popular)
- A finger brush or small dog toothbrush
- Never use human toothpaste — fluoride and xylitol are toxic to dogs

### Step-by-Step
1. Start by letting your pug lick the toothpaste from your finger — get them comfortable with the taste
2. Apply paste to the brush/finger brush
3. Gently lift the lip and brush in small circular motions along the gumline
4. Focus on the outer surfaces of the back teeth — that's where tartar builds fastest
5. Work up to 2 minutes total over time; start with whatever they'll tolerate

**Dental chews** like Greenies can supplement brushing but don't replace it. Choose ones sized appropriately for small breeds.

**Annual professional cleaning:** Even with daily brushing, pugs need professional scaling under anesthesia starting at 1–2 years old. Schedule this with your vet as a routine annual event.

## Coat Care and Managing Shedding

Pugs have a dense double coat that sheds year-round. Spring and fall bring heavy seasonal shedding that can be genuinely surprising if you're not prepared.

### Brushing
**Best tools:**
- Rubber curry brush (most effective for short coats)
- Hound mitt (good for sensitive-skinned pugs)
- Zoom Groom or similar rubber nub brush

**Technique:**
1. Brush in the direction of hair growth
2. Use firm but gentle circular motions to loosen dead coat
3. Follow with a lint roll or rubber glove to pick up loose hair
4. Brush outside if possible during peak shedding — you'll generate a surprising amount of loose fur

**During shedding season (spring/fall):** Brush every day. Consider a deshedding tool like the Furminator (use gently — overuse can irritate the skin) once or twice a week during peak periods.

### Managing Shedding in the Home
- Sofa covers and throws (easy to wash)
- A good vacuum with pet hair attachments
- Rubber gloves — running a damp rubber glove over furniture picks up embedded hair remarkably well
- HEPA air purifier in rooms where your pug spends the most time

## Bathing

### How Often
Every 3–4 weeks, or as needed. Over-bathing strips the coat's natural oils and dries out the skin.

### Products
- Gentle, dog-safe shampoo formulated for sensitive skin (pugs often have sensitive skin)
- Optional: dog conditioner for coat shine

### Step-by-Step
1. Brush your pug thoroughly before bathing to remove loose hair
2. Use lukewarm water — never hot
3. Wet the coat thoroughly, avoiding getting water in ears and eyes
4. Apply shampoo and lather gently, working it into all wrinkles
5. Rinse completely — residual shampoo causes skin irritation
6. **Dry wrinkles thoroughly** before they close back up
7. Use a low-heat blow dryer or dry thoroughly with towels — a damp coat in folds causes skin infections

## Ear Cleaning

### How Often
Every 1–2 weeks for most pugs. More frequently for those prone to ear infections.

### What You'll Need
- Vet-approved ear cleaning solution
- Cotton balls or gauze (never cotton swabs)

### Step-by-Step
1. Fill the ear canal with cleaning solution
2. Gently massage the base of the ear for 20–30 seconds — you'll hear a squishing sound
3. Let your pug shake their head
4. Wipe the outer ear canal and ear flap with a cotton ball
5. Never push anything down into the ear canal

**Signs of ear infection:** head shaking, scratching at ears, dark brown/black discharge, foul odor, redness. See your vet.

## Nail Trimming

Most pugs dislike nail trims intensely. Start handling their paws from puppyhood to desensitize.

### How Often
Every 2–3 weeks. Nails that are too long click on hard floors and can alter gait, eventually causing joint problems.

### Tools
- Guillotine-style or scissor-style clippers for small breeds
- Styptic powder (to stop bleeding if you nick the quick)
- A nail file for smoothing

### Step-by-Step
1. Hold the paw firmly but gently
2. Identify the quick — the pink vein visible in light-colored nails. In dark nails, trim small amounts at a time and look for a dark circle in the center of the cut surface
3. Clip just the tip, avoiding the quick
4. File any sharp edges
5. Reward with treats throughout

**If your pug is very resistant:** Try counter-conditioning — touch paws during TV time and treat, long before any trimming. Gradual desensitization works.

## Building a Grooming Routine

Pugs that are groomed consistently from puppyhood tolerate it far better than those who only experience it occasionally. Make grooming a regular, calm, positive experience:

- Keep sessions short in the beginning
- Use high-value treats throughout
- Always end on a positive note
- Same time, same place helps pugs predict and accept the routine

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you clean a pug's nose wrinkle without causing irritation?**
Use gentle, unscented dog-safe wipes or a soft damp cloth. Avoid alcohol-based products. Always dry the fold completely after cleaning.

**Do pugs need professional grooming?**
Not strictly necessary, but a professional groomer every 6–8 weeks can handle deshedding treatments, nail grinding (smoother than clipping), and a thorough bath. Many owners find this a useful supplement to their home routine.

**Why does my pug smell even after a bath?**
Usually it's the wrinkles. If folds aren't dried thoroughly after bathing, yeast multiplies quickly and produces odor. Clean and dry each fold again after bathing, and consider whether you need a more frequent wrinkle-cleaning routine.

**How do I stop my pug from hating nail trims?**
Start very young, go very slowly, and use extremely high-value treats. Consider nail grinding instead of clipping (many dogs find the vibration less startling than the snap of clippers). Ask your vet or a groomer to show you the technique if you're nervous about cutting the quick.
